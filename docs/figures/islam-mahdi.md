# Islam: Mahdi

The **Mahdi** (مهدي, "guided one") is a **future restorer** of justice in Sunni and especially Shīʿī eschatologies, with hadith reports gathered from the 7th–9th centuries CE onward (compilation dates ≠ origin of every report).

## Dating and sources

| Layer | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hadith corpora | ~7th–9th cent. CE (compilation) | Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Sunan Abū Dāwūd, etc. |
| Shīʿī elaboration | Later 1st millennium CE onward | Imāmī and other Shīʿī traditions; occultation of the Twelfth Imam interacts with Mahdi expectation |

## Role and motifs

- **Justice** on earth before Judgment Day; often paired with ʿĪsā (Jesus) returning to kill the Dajjāl (Antichrist figure) in many Sunni narratives — distinct from Christian second coming theology but comparatively notable.
- **Genealogy**: often descent from the Prophet (details vary by school).

## Scholarly notes

Comparative studies (e.g. Mahdi and Maitreya) stress differences in nonviolence vs. militant justice tropes as much as similarities. Political Mahdi movements have recurred across Islamic history.

## Tier statement

| Claim | Tier | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mahdi expectation as attested in Sunni hadith corpora | **Tier A** | Documented in Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Sunan Abū Dāwūd, and related collections |
| Twelver Shia identification of the Mahdi with the Hidden Twelfth Imam | **Tier A** | Core Imāmī doctrine; extensively documented in specialist literature |
| Functional structural parallel with Maitreya, Kalki, and Saoshyant as future restorers | **Tier B** | Shared functional slot; no documented genealogical connection between the traditions |
| Iranian Zoroastrian eschatology influencing early Islamic Mahdi expectations | **Tier C** | Plausible given Sassanid Persian cultural context; mechanism and dating debated |

## Sources & dating

### Primary evidence

| Witness / work | Language | Approx. date | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim (relevant hadith) | Arabic | ~9th cent. CE (compiled) | Standard Sunni hadith collection; Siddiqui translation |
| Sunan Abū Dāwūd | Arabic | ~9th cent. CE (compiled) | Mahdi-related hadith; Ahmad Hasan translation |
| Shīʿī hadith (Kāfī, Biḥār al-Anwār) | Arabic | 9th–17th cent. CE (compiled) | Twelver Imāmī sources on the Hidden Imam / Mahdi; Majlisi's *Biḥār al-Anwār* is the major later compilation |

### Tier B — specialist analyses

- **Madelung, Wilferd.** "Mahdī." *Encyclopaedia Iranica* — typology and early Shiʿite development; the standard reference entry.
- **Arjomand, Said Amir.** *The Shadow of God and the Hidden Imam.* Chicago — Imāmī political messianism (historical sociology).
- **Cook, David.** *Studies in Muslim Apocalyptic.* Darwin Press — hadith and apocalyptic themes (use critically; specialist debates on dating and authenticity of individual reports).

### Standard reference works

- **Encyclopaedia of Islam** (Brill, 2nd ed.) — *al-Mahdī* — comprehensive bibliographic overview.
