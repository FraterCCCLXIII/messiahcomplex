# Elijah and Enoch

**Elijah** (Hebrew: אֵלִיָּהוּ, *Eliyyahu*) and **Enoch** (חֲנוֹךְ, *Ḥanokh*) are the two biblical figures who are taken to heaven without dying. This exceptional status made them the primary candidates for **eschatological return** in Jewish, Christian, and later Islamic tradition — a role that directly intersects with messianic expectation.

## Dating and sources

| Source | Approximate date | Content |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Genesis 5:24** | Pentateuchal composition (Iron Age) | "Enoch walked with God, and he was not, for God took him" |
| **1 Kings 17–19; 2 Kings 1–2** | 9th–7th cent. BCE (Deuteronomistic History) | Elijah cycle: miracles, confrontation with Baal, chariot of fire, ascent |
| **Malachi 3:23–24** (= 4:5–6 in English) | ~5th cent. BCE | "I will send you Elijah the prophet before the great and terrible day of the LORD" |
| **Sirach (Ben Sira) 48:1–12** | ~180 BCE | Elijah's eschatological role; "designated to reprove at the appointed time" |
| **1 Enoch** (Ethiopic Enoch) | 3rd cent. BCE – 1st cent. CE (composite) | Enoch as visionary, scribe of heaven, mediator of divine secrets; "Son of Man" passages (Similitudes) |
| **2 Enoch** (Slavonic Enoch) | 1st cent. CE? (debated) | Enoch's heavenly ascent; cosmology; calendar |
| **Jubilees 4:23** | 2nd cent. BCE | Enoch in Eden, writing testimony |
| **Synoptic Gospels** (Mark 6:15; 8:28; 9:2–13) | 1st cent. CE | Jesus identified as Elijah returned; Transfiguration with Moses and Elijah |
| **Revelation 11:3–12** | Late 1st cent. CE | "Two witnesses" who prophesy and are killed, then resurrected — traditionally identified as Moses & Elijah or Enoch & Elijah |
| **Quran** (Idrīs, 19:56–57; 21:85–86) | 7th cent. CE | Idrīs (commonly identified with Enoch): "raised to a high station" |

## Role and motifs

### Elijah

| Motif | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Prophet vs. false cult** | Confrontation with Baal prophets at Mt. Carmel (1 Kings 18) |
| **Miracle worker** | Resurrects the widow's son (1 Kings 17:17–24); multiplies food; controls rain |
| **Ascent without death** | Taken up in a whirlwind with chariots of fire (2 Kings 2:11) |
| **Eschatological forerunner** | Returns before the Day of the LORD (Malachi); precursor to the Messiah in rabbinic and Christian tradition |
| **Ongoing presence** | In Jewish practice: chair of Elijah at circumcision, cup at Passover seder — Elijah as the liminal visitor |
| **Identification with Jesus** | Multiple NT passages report popular belief that Jesus was Elijah returned; Jesus reinterprets John the Baptist as Elijah (Matt 11:14; 17:12) |

### Enoch

| Motif | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| **Taken by God** | Genesis 5:24; no death recorded |
| **Heavenly scribe** | In 1 Enoch and Jubilees: records divine judgments; tours heavenly realms |
| **Revealer of secrets** | Angelic guides show him cosmic architecture, calendar, and eschatological future |
| **"Son of Man" association** | 1 Enoch Similitudes (37–71): the Elect One / Son of Man — some scholars read Enoch himself as identified with this figure |
| **Metatron** | In later Jewish mysticism (3 Enoch / Hekhalot literature): Enoch transformed into the archangel Metatron, "lesser YHWH" |

## Cross-tradition parallels

- **Zoroastrian Saoshyant**: future savior whose arrival is preceded by cosmic signs; Elijah as forerunner parallels the Zoroastrian pattern of eschatological preparation. See [Saoshyant](zoroastrian-saoshyant.md).
- **Khiḍr (Islamic tradition)**: an immortal figure who wanders the earth, sometimes identified with or paralleled to Elijah; both serve as guides and protectors who appear unexpectedly
- **Ascended masters / translated heroes**: Enoch's bodily ascent parallels Heracles' apotheosis, Romulus's disappearance, and the Buddha's parinirvāṇa — different theological frameworks for "the extraordinary human who doesn't simply die"
- **Angelomorphic mediators**: Enoch's transformation into Metatron parallels the development of named angelic hierarchies in post-exilic Judaism — see [Jewish angelology and yazata parallels](../overview/jewish-angelology-and-yazata-parallels.md)
- **The "two witnesses" pattern** (Revelation 11): pairs of returning eschatological figures — compare the Zoroastrian pattern of multiple Saoshyants

## Scholarly notes

Elijah and Enoch are important test cases for the site's Persian corridor thesis. The eschatological Elijah (Malachi, Sirach) develops in the **post-exilic period** — exactly the era of Achaemenid cultural influence. The Enochic literature (especially 1 Enoch) shows a dramatic expansion of cosmological and angelological imagination that scholars have compared to Iranian models (Hultgård, Collins). Whether these developments reflect direct Persian influence, shared ANE heritage, or independent Jewish innovation remains debated — but the chronological alignment with Persian contact is significant.

The Enoch tradition is also relevant for understanding how Jewish eschatology moved from prophetic "Day of the LORD" language toward **apocalypticism** — a shift that many scholars associate (at least partly) with exposure to Zoroastrian dualism and eschatology during and after the exile.
