# Buddhism: Maitreya (Metteyya)

**Maitreya** (Sanskrit) / **Metteyya** (Pāli) is the **future Buddha** who will arise when Śākyamuni’s dispensation has long declined and been renewed.

## Dating and sources

| Milestone | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Earliest attestation** | ~3rd cent. BCE (canon fixed over time) | **Dīgha Nikāya 26**, *Cakkavatti-Sīhanāda Sutta* |
| Mahāyāna expansion | 1st millennium CE onward | Maitreya sūtras, śāstras (e.g. **Asanga**), art across Asia |

In DN 26, the Buddha prophesies **Metteyya** as a future Buddha after a long period of moral collapse and recovery; the sutta ties this to **cakravartin** (wheel-turning) kingship imagery and an extraordinary **future age** (long lifespans, Ketumatī, etc.). Modern scholarship often treats this as the **first clear textual mention** of Metteyya.

## Role and motifs

- **Bodhisattva** trajectory in Mahāyāna: Maitreya dwells in **Tuṣita** heaven before descent.
- **Dharma** restoration—not a Davidic or Iranian king in the biblical sense, but a **parallel** in “future restorer of the true teaching.”

## Scholarly notes

**Indo-Iranian influence** on the Maitreya motif is **debated**; some see distant echoes, others stress **indigenous** Buddhist development. Compare Islamic **Mahdi** and Jewish **messiah** in comparative studies (e.g. *Religions* 2024 on savior narratives).
