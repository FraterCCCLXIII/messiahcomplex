# Taoism: Li Hong (李弘)

**Li Hong** is a **messianic savior-king** figure in **Chinese Daoist eschatology**, especially associated with **Shangqing** (Highest Clarity) and broader **Taiping** (Great Peace) expectations.

## Dating and sources

| Layer | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prophetic / popular lore | Han and earlier echoes | Apocrypha, portents, surname **Li** as sign |
| Shangqing revelations | **364–370 CE** | **Zhen’gao** (*Declarations of the Perfected*), recorded via **Yang Xi**; compiled later (e.g. **Tao Hongjing**) |

The **Zhen’gao** material gives Li Hong a **cosmic** role: arrival amid **chaos**, renewal of **heaven and earth**, rescue of the **“seed people”** (zhongmin), and restoration of **harmony**—often linked to **Laozi** through the **Li** surname and **incarnation/emanation** themes.

## Role and motifs

- **End-of-kalpa** style crisis + **sage-king** restoration (terminology overlaps Buddhist **kalpa** discourse in Chinese religion).
- Political resonance: many **rebellions** used Li Hong messianism.

## Scholarly notes

Dating **“first attestation”** to the **4th century CE** follows explicit **Shangqing** revelation texts; **earlier** Han motifs may be **precursors**. Consult **Daoist studies** specialists for philology and social history.
