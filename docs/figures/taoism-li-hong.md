# Taoism: Li Hong (李弘)

**Li Hong** is a messianic savior-king figure in Chinese Daoist eschatology, especially associated with **Shangqing** (Highest Clarity) and broader **Taiping** (Great Peace) expectations. The figure sits at the intersection of cosmological prophecy, political legitimation, and popular rebellion — making it the comparative site's clearest East Asian instance of an empire-crisis-messiah dynamic operating independently of the Western Eurasian axis.

## Dating and sources

| Layer | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Prophetic / popular lore | Han and earlier echoes | Apocrypha (chenwei), portent literature; surname Li as sign of dynastic legitimacy |
| Shangqing revelations | 364–370 CE | Zhen'gao (*Declarations of the Perfected*), recorded via Yang Xi; compiled later by Tao Hongjing (~500 CE) |

The Zhen'gao material gives Li Hong a cosmic role: arrival amid chaos, renewal of heaven and earth, rescue of the "seed people" (zhongmin), and restoration of harmony — often linked to Laozi through the Li surname and incarnation/emanation themes.

## Role and motifs

- **End-of-kalpa** style crisis followed by sage-king restoration (terminology overlaps Buddhist kalpa discourse in Chinese religion, reflecting the Buddhist-Daoist contact zone of the 4th–6th centuries CE).
- Political resonance: multiple rebellions across Chinese history deployed Li Hong messianism as legitimating narrative.

## Scholarly notes

Dating "first attestation" to the 4th century CE follows explicit Shangqing revelation texts; earlier Han motifs may be precursors. The political deployment of the Li Hong figure across Tang, Song, and later dynasties shows that messianic expectation in Daoism was not a marginal phenomenon but a recurring feature of popular religion under imperial pressure.

## Sources & dating

### Primary evidence

| Witness / work | Language | Approx. date | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Zhen'gao (真誥, *Declarations of the Perfected*) | Classical Chinese | Revelations 364–370 CE; compiled ~500 CE | Yang Xi's revelations, compiled and annotated by Tao Hongjing; Bokenkamp's *Early Daoist Scriptures* provides introduction and partial translation |
| Taishang Dongyuan Shenzhou Jing and related Taiping texts | Classical Chinese | Han period – 2nd cent. CE (debated) | Prophetic texts with Li Hong precursor motifs; Schipper & Verellen, *The Taoist Canon* for orientation |

### Tier B — specialist analyses

- **Bokenkamp, Stephen R.** *Early Daoist Scriptures.* California — *Zhen'gao* and related Shangqing texts with introduction; essential starting point.
- **Kohn, Livia.** *Daoism and Chinese Culture.* Three Pines Press — Shangqing and messianic themes accessible overview.
- **Robinet, Isabelle.** *Taoism: Growth of a Religion.* Stanford (tr.) — historical layers of Daoist messianism.
- **Strickmann, Michel.** *Chinese Magical Medicine.* Stanford — related ritual and revelation context.

### Standard reference works

- **Schipper, Kristofer & Franciscus Verellen (eds.).** *The Taoist Canon: A Historical Companion.* Chicago — guide to the Daozang with dating assessments.
