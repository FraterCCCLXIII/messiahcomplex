# Roman Mithraism: Mithras

**Mithraism** in the Roman Empire was a **mystery cult** centered on **Mithras** (not identical text-for-text with **Mitra** / **Mithra** in Iranian sources). Its **tauroctony** iconography and **initiatory** soteriology flourished from roughly the **1st–4th centuries CE**.

## Dating and sources

| Evidence | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Literary mentions | ~1st cent. CE | **Plutarch**, **Statius**, etc. |
| Archaeology | ~67 BCE onward (disputed earliest monuments) | **Tauroctony** reliefs, *mithraea* |

## Role and motifs

- **Salvation** through graded initiation, cosmic imagery, **bull-slaying** as generative/sacrificial act (interpretations vary).
- **Oaths and bonds:** initiates encountered **passwords**, **stages**, and **cosmic** imagery that **rhyme** with older **Indo-Iranian** **contract/witness** themes — without settling how much is **Roman invention** vs **Iranian** **memory** (see [Indo-Iranian Mitra and the contract complex](../overview/indo-iranian-mitra-contract.md)).
- **Not** primarily a **future eschatological** savior like the **Saoshyant** or **Mahdi**; emphasis is often on **present** cultic mediation and **afterlife** hope.

## Scholarly notes

Iranian **continuity** vs. **Roman innovation** is debated (**Cumont**’s classic Iranian theory vs **Beck** / **Clauss** on **syncretic** creation); parallels to **Zoroastrian** eschatology are **motivic** (sacrifice, cosmic order) rather than a simple import of **Saoshyant** doctrine. **Manichaean** **Mihr** offers yet another Iranian-tinged **light-mediator** pattern — [Roman Mithras and Manichaean Mithra](../overview/mithra-roman-manichaean.md), [Manichaean Mihr and soteriology](../overview/manichaean-mihr-soteriology.md).

Listing Mithras here highlights **family resemblances** in “savior” language while marking **category differences**.

### Secondary entrances

- **Beck, Roger.** “The Mysteries of Mithras: A New Account of Their Symbolism.” *Journal of Roman Studies* 88 (1998): 115–128 — influential symbolic reading.
- **Beck, Roger.** *The Religion of the Mithras Cult in the Roman Empire.* Oxford University Press, 2006 — astral theology; companion to Clauss.
- **Clauss, Manfred.** *The Roman Cult of Mithras: The God and His Mysteries.* Routledge, 2000 (tr.) — the primary modern handbook.
- **Walsh, David.** Dissertation on Mithraic decline — multi-factor archaeological analysis of mithraeum abandonment (Constantine’s patronage of Christianity, anti-Persian sentiment, barbarian destruction of frontier sites, voluntary abandonment).
- **Gordon, Richard L.** “Who Worshipped Mithras?” *Journal of Roman Archaeology* 7 (1994): 459–474 — social composition: male-only, military concentration, stratified grades.
- **Ulansey, David.** *The Origins of the Mithraic Mysteries.* Oxford, 1989 — astrological thesis (debated).
- **Higgins, Godfrey.** *Anacalypsis* (1836) — **not a source on Mithraism**; 19th-century pseudo-history; origin of the false “twelve disciples” claim with no ancient support.
- **Doane, T. W.** *Bible Myths and Their Parallels in Other Religions* (1882) — **not a source on Mithraism**; origin of false Magi/Mithras claim with no ancient support.
