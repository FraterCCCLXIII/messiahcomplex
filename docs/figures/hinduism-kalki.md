# Hinduism: Kalki

**Kalki** is the **tenth avatāra** of Viṣṇu in many Vaishnava lists — the eschatological warrior who ends the **Kali Yuga** and restores **dharma**.

## Dating and sources

| Layer | Approximate date | Sources |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early references | Epic core often placed ~400 BCE – 200 CE (debated) | Mahābhārata (e.g. 3.188–189) |
| Detailed mythology | 1st millennium CE and later | Purāṇas (Viṣṇu, Bhāgavata, Bhaviṣya, etc.); Kalki Purāṇa (much later, regional/textual history complex) |

Purāṇic accounts supply **white horse**, **sword**, birthplace (Śambhala-type motifs vary), and battle against **adharma**.

## Role and motifs

- **Cosmic cycle**: Kali Yuga's darkness yields to Satya Yuga renewal — cyclical time, unlike linear Frashokereti or single Parousia in other systems.
- Visual culture: **horse**, **weapon**, kingly presence.

## Scholarly notes

Dating epic and Purāṇic layers is contested; "first attestation" in the table reflects Mahābhārata references, not the full Kalki Purāṇa legend. Comparisons to Saoshyant or Maitreya are analogical — Hindu avatāra theology remains distinct.

## Tier statement

| Claim | Tier | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Kalki as Viṣṇu's tenth avatāra in Purāṇic tradition | **Tier A** | Textually secure across multiple Purāṇas; iconographic tradition well established |
| Mahābhārata as the earliest textual reference to the Kalki figure | **Tier B** | Epic dating is debated (~400 BCE – 400 CE); the Kalki passages may be among later layers |
| Cyclical-time framework (Kali Yuga → Satya Yuga) as structurally distinct from linear Zoroastrian or Abrahamic eschatology | **Tier A** | Structural observation; consensus in comparative religion |
| Structural parallel to Saoshyant, Maitreya, and Mahdi as future restorers | **Tier D** | Analogy only; no documented channel of transmission |

See [Method: evidence, influence, and milieu](../overview/method-evidence-and-influence.md) for tier definitions.

## Sources & dating

### Primary evidence

| Witness / work | Language | Approx. date | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mahābhārata (Book 3, Vanaparva) | Sanskrit | ~400 BCE – 400 CE (compiled; debated) | Critical edition: Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI); van Buitenen translation (Chicago) |
| Viṣṇu Purāṇa | Sanskrit | ~400–1000 CE (debated) | Standard avatāra lists; Murty Classical Library editions preferred over older Wilson translation |
| Bhāgavata Purāṇa (Book 12) | Sanskrit | ~9th–10th cent. CE | Detailed Kalki narrative; Bryant translation (Columbia) |
| Kalki Purāṇa | Sanskrit | Late medieval (date complex) | Regional elaboration; no standard critical edition; treat with source caution |

### Tier B — specialist analyses

- **Doniger, Wendy** (O'Flaherty). *The Origins of Evil in Hindu Mythology.* California — eschatological themes and yuga cosmology in context.
- **Hiltebeitel, Alf.** *Rethinking the Mahābhārata.* Chicago — epic stratification and dating arguments.
- **Gonzalez-Reimann, Luis.** *The Mahābhārata and the Yugas.* Lang — yuga chronology debates; essential for assessing the Kalki passage dates.

### Standard reference works

- **Encyclopaedia of Religion** (Eliade, ed.) — *Avatāra*; *Eschatology: Hindu concepts* — overview entries with bibliography.
