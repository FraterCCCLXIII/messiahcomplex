# Mithra cluster hub

## Context

- **Audience:** Readers tracing **Indo-Iranian** *\*Mitra-*, **Roman** mystery **Mithras**, **Manichaean** **Mihr**, and **Buddhist** **Maitreya** / Pure Land material in one frame—without equating names or ignoring chronology.
- **Question:** Where is **contact** plausible, where is **motif** only, and where is **etymology** still disputed? Child pages separate **linguistics**, **art**, and **theology**.
- **Non-goals:** This hub does not prove that all “Mithra-like” figures are one god; it maps **evidence tiers** and **transmission** corridors (see [Measuring influence](overview/measuring-influence-mitra-maitreya.md)).

The **Mithra dossier** develops a **“divine contract”** line of argument: **Indo-Iranian** *\*Mitra-* (“covenant, bond, oath-guarantor”) as a lens on **Zoroastrian Mithra**, **Buddhist Maitreya** (and related **Pure Land** material), **Jewish covenantal prayer**, and **Roman / Manichaean** Mithras-forms. The **editorial stance** here treats that cluster as **historically plausible at the level of contact and motif**, while separating **strong** evidence (e.g. Mitanni and Avestan **contract-god** Mithra; **Mithredath** as Persian **theophoric** name in Ezra) from **weak or contested** jumps (e.g. genetic **Hebrew עמד** — “stand” — cognate with *\*mitra-*, or **identity** of Mithra and Maitreya).

**Curated pages** (argument + caution) sit below; the **raw compilation** remains available for quotations and leads.
