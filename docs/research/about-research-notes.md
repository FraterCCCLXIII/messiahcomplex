# Research folder: conventions

The **research** folder under `docs/research/` holds **working** and **raw** material that may include **mixed** **provenance** (notes, quotations, forum excerpts, speculative chains).

## Curated vs raw

- **Curated** pages live under **overview** and **figures** in the docs plugin. They follow the site’s **editorial** tone and should **signal** **evidence** tier — see [Method: evidence, influence, and milieu](../overview/method-evidence-and-influence.md). For **archives, onomastics, and Persian–Jewish interface** leads (separate from raw Mithra notes), see [Evidence leads — Persian milieu and Mitra cluster](../overview/evidence-leads-persian-milieu-and-mitra-cluster.md).
- **Raw** compilations are **not** peer review. Treat **claims** there as **leads** until checked against **editions** and **specialist** literature.

## Tools, gaps, and humility

Search engines, digital indexes, and large language models can help cluster motifs, draft timelines, and suggest etymological hypotheses. Outputs remain hypothesis generators until verified against primary texts and scholarly debate (see [Method: evidence, influence, and milieu](../overview/method-evidence-and-influence.md) on assisted collation).

Most ancient religious material is lost or survives in late manuscripts; what we read today is often a small fraction of what was once written or recited. Comparative work should assume large unknowns under the waterline—not treat every gap as permission to assert a specific hidden history.

## When to add a new raw file

Use a **new** compilation when:

- a topic **outgrows** footnotes on a **curated** page, and
- **mixed-quality** **snippets** would **clutter** the public article.

Link the curated page **prominently** to the raw dump and **summarize** only **verified** **points** in the curated layer.

## Sources and dating (required for non-stub pages)

Curated **figure** and **overview** pages should include a **Sources & dating** section with:

1. **Primary witnesses** — language, approximate date, standard edition or corpus siglum where applicable.  
2. **Major translations** — if the audience reads primarily English (or note “Greek/Latin only”).  
3. **Secondary entrances** — at least two scholarly handbooks, monographs, or encyclopedia articles with full citation lines.

Use the **[Curated page template](../overview/curated-page-template.md)** (copy-paste block + hub strategy). Prefer real bibliographic data over placeholders.
