# Assessment: Eurasian gods, demigods, and heroes for this thesis

This page answers: **which** figures **belong** in a project that maps **messianic and cognate archetypes**—**future saviors**, **cosmic renovators**, **initiators**, **healing mediators**, **solar/exalted lords**, and **hero-cult “saviors”**—without **flattening** distinct theologies into one story.

**Tiers** here are a **map for exploration**—which complexes deserve articles and cross-links—not a fence around curiosity. For **how** we hunt **actual** connections (mechanisms, falsifiers), see [Exploring transmission and deep narrative patterns](exploring-transmission-and-deep-patterns.md).

## Inclusion criteria (functional, not identity)

A name earns a place if it **performs one or more** of these **roles** in attested **cult**, **myth**, or **art**:

| Role type | Illustration |
| --- | --- |
| **Eschatological / future** restorer | Saoshyant, Mahdi, Maitreya, Kalki (tradition-specific) |
| **Sōtēr-type** mediator (healing, fate, grave hope) | Asclepius, Thracian **Hērōs**, Isis in some periods |
| **Mystery initiator** | Dionysus, Attis, Cybele, Eleusinian gods, Mithras |
| **Solar / celestial sovereign** (often synced) | Helios, **Sol**, **Apollo–Helios**, late **Sol Invictus** |
| **Dying–rising** or **seasonal** renewal god | Osiris, Adonis, Attis, Dumuzi (careful: not all “same myth”) |
| **Wandering / teaching** divine or hero | Hermes, Apollo, Dionysus, regional **Asclepius** epiphany |
| **Imperial** **soter** idiom | Living ruler as **θεός** / **savior**; **dating by** salvation epithets |

**Exclusion** from *equal* treatment with **Jewish messiah** does **not** mean **exclusion** from the **map**: most entries are **analogues** or **milieu**, not **genealogical** ancestors of **Christology**.

**Roman vs Greek names:** **Bacchus** is the **Latin** / poetic byname for **Dionysus**; analytically one **complex**, two **registers**.
