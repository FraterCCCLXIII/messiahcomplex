# PIE & ANE deep grammar

## Context

- **Audience:** Readers asking how the oldest recoverable narrative and linguistic layers — Proto-Indo-European reconstructions and Ancient Near Eastern religious literature — feed into the later, historically attested messianic traditions.
- **Question:** How deep does the shared grammar go, and at what point does "parallel" become "inheritance"?
- **Scope:** This zone covers (1) Proto-Indo-European narrative reconstructions; (2) Ancient Near Eastern mythology in the Hebrew Bible; (3) Paleo-Balkan horseman and hero cult. It is explicitly the **most hypothesis-heavy** part of the site — reconstructions require checking against philology and archaeology. See [Method: evidence, influence, and milieu](method-evidence-and-influence.md).

---

## What this zone argues

The historically attested messianic traditions are not free-floating inventions. They draw on **narrative grammar** that is older than any of them — story slots, sacrificial logic, and cosmological structures that recur across cousin cultures for documentable reasons.

**Three layers are distinguished:**

1. **Proto-Indo-European reconstruction** — linguistic and comparative evidence for narrative patterns shared across the IE language family before any attested text. The *\*Manu / \*Yemo* "primal twins" reconstruction is the best example: one twin founds humanity; the other is sacrificed to create the world. Reflexes appear in Indian *Manu / Yama*, Iranian *Manuš / Yima*, Norse *Mannus / Ymir*, and Roman *Romulus / Remus*. This is **hypothesis-tier** reconstruction, not Bronze Age source text.

2. **ANE parallels to Biblical tradition** — the Hebrew Bible exhibits substantial overlap with Ugaritic, Canaanite, and Mesopotamian mythological literature: the storm-god combat (Yamm/Leviathan), the council of the gods (Psalm 82), the Baal cycle's motifs. These are partly direct borrowing (Canaanite literature was the immediate literary environment of early Hebrew scribes) and partly shared West Semitic inheritance — not evidence of IE origin.

3. **Paleo-Balkan horseman cult** — the Thracian *Hērōs* (the unnamed rider, inscribed as *sōtēr* and *epekoos* in Latin and Greek) sits in the same Balkan environment as early Christian mission sites (Macedonia, Philippi, Thessalonica). It is a **type site** for how "horseman + savior" language circulated in the pre-Christian Mediterranean.
