# Attis and Cybele (Magna Mater)

**Cybele** (**Κυβέλη**, **Magna Mater**) is an **Anatolian** **Great Goddess** whose cult **spread** through **Greece** and, spectacularly, **Rome** from the **Republic** onward. **Attis** is her **consort** figure: **castration**, **death**, and **vegetal** **renewal** dominate **myth** and **April** / **March** **festival** cycles in **literary** and **epigraphic** **reception**. The pair belongs in this project as a major **mystery** and **“dying–renewing”** **idiom** in **shared** **urban** space with **early Christianity**—**not** as proof of **literary** **dependence**.

## Names and geography

- **Cybele**: linked to **Phrygia** / **Anatolia**; **Pessinous** looms large in **Roman** import **stories** (black stone; **Claudian** narrative layers).
- **Attis**: **shepherd** / **beloved** / **consort** roles vary by **period** and **source**; **Roman** state **calendar** integrates **rites** (e.g. **Megalesia**, **Hilaria** in later **imperial** reception).
- **Priests**: **galli** (**eunuch** priests), **Archigallus** under **imperial** organization—**gender** and **ritual** **liminality** are central to **ancient** **Christian** polemic as well as **modern** **gender** studies.

## Myth and ritual (simplified)

Scholarship stresses **stratification**: **Hellenistic** and **Roman** **layers** **reinterpret** older **Anatolian** material.

- **Pine** **tree**, **blood** / **sacrifice**, and **spring** **vegetation** **logic** tie Attis to **renewal** without requiring a **single** “**resurrected** god” **template** for all **Mediterranean** cults.
- **Taurobolium** / **criobolium** (bull or ram **rites**) are often associated with **Cybele** and **related** **Meter** cult in **Roman** cities; **interpretations** range from **purification** to **initiation** to **status** display—**avoid** collapsing them into **Christian** **baptism** by **analogy** alone.

**Literary** **anchors** (**Pausanias** *Description of Greece*; **Diodorus** *Bibliotheca historica*) **report** **Phrygian** **myth** **and** **ritual** **through** **Greek** **ethnographic** **lenses**—[Appendix — Mediterranean mystery sources](mediterranean-mystery-sources-appendix.md).

## Mediator and “savior” vocabulary

- **Cybele**: **protectress** of **city** and **empire**; **epithets** and **iconography** stress **majesty** and **fierce** **care**.
- **Attis**: **initiatory** **belonging** and **funerary** associations appear in **evidence** for **collegia** and **mystery** **language**; **explicit** *sōtēr* **parity** with **Asclepius** or **Isis** is **not** uniform—**role** is often **renewal** and **seasonal** **rite** rather than **personal** **eschatological** **savior**.
