# Appendix: Mediterranean mystery sources (orientation)

This page **surveys** **types** of **evidence** for **hymns**, **oracles**, **initiation** **language**, and **ritual** **reports** in **Greek** and **Roman** **religion**, with emphasis on **Apollo**, **Helios**/**Sol**, **Hermes**, **Dionysus**, **Demeter**/**Eleusis**, and **Orphic**/**Bacchic** **strands**. It supports [Greek wandering gods](greek-wandering-gods-initiation.md), [Helios, Apollo, and Sol](helios-apollo-sol-syncretism.md), and related **figure** pages.

**Scope:** **not** a catalogue of every **inscription**; **not** a discussion of **Abrahamic**, **Buddhist**, or **Vedic** **canon** history (see tradition-specific **figure** pages and **handbooks**). **Dates** for **anonymous** or **composite** texts (e.g. *Orphic Hymns*) are **debated**—check **current** **editions**.

## 1. No single “Apolline” or “Sol” scripture

Unlike the **Corpus Hermeticum** (late antique **Hermetica** under the name **Hermes Trismegistus**), **Apollo** and **Roman Sol** **lack** one **surviving** **corpus** that **only** **treats** either **god**. **Traditions** are **dispersed**:

| Channel | Examples (illustrative) |
| --- | --- |
| **Oracular** **sites** | **Delphi**, **Didyma**: **responses** recorded on **perishable** **media** and in **literary** **quotation**; **interpretation** varies by **period**. |
| **Sibylline** **idiom** | **Roman** **Sibylline** **Books** (linked in **tradition** to **prophetic** **Sibyls** **associated** with **Apollo**) — **lost** in **antiquity**; **not** **reconstructible** as a **single** **archive**. |
| **Mythography** | **Pseudo-Apollodorus**, *Library* (Imperial **prose** **compendium** of **myths** **bearing** on **Apollo** and **others**). |
| **Hymnic** **poetry** | **Homeric Hymns** (incl. **Apollo**); **Callimachus**, *Hymn to Apollo* (Hellenistic). **Orphic**-**labeled** **hymns** to **Apollo** appear in **debated** **collections**—see §4. |
| **Sol** **in** **Rome** | **Calendar** **evidence** (e.g. **Chronograph** of **354** / **Philocalian** **calendar**, **Dies** **Natalis** **Solis** **Invicti** — **interpretation** of **“Invictus”** and **December** **25** **belongs** to **specialist** **discussion**); **inscriptions** and **imperial** **titulature** rather than a **fixed** **liturgical** **book**. |
| **Drama** **and** **philosophy** | **Tragedy** (**Sophocles** *Oedipus*; **Euripides** *Alcestis*); **Neoplatonists** citing **oracles** (**Iamblichus**, **Proclus**) — **theological** **reuse**, **not** **temple** **archives**. |
| **Epigraphy** | **Temple** **dedications**, **curse** **tablets**, **honorific** **texts** — **local** and **dated** per **corpus**. |

**Editorial point:** **absence** of a **Hermetica**-**style** **codex** for **Apollo**/**Sol** is **compatible** with **strong** **cult** **and** **art** **evidence**; it does **not** imply **weak** **public** **presence**.

## 2. Journeys, mediators, and “teaching” gods (selected literary anchors)

These **texts** **illustrate** **wandering**, **foundation**, or **psychopomp** **roles** used on [Greek wandering gods](greek-wandering-gods-initiation.md); they **do** **not** **prove** **genealogical** **links** to **later** **messianic** **doctrine**.

| Theme | Sources (examples) |
| --- | --- |
| **Hermes** **as** **trickster**-**mediator**; **sacrifice** **aetiology** | *Homeric Hymn to Hermes* (archaic **hymn** **cycle**). |
| **Apollo** **and** **Hyperborea** | **Pindar** (*Pythians*); **Herodotus** (ethnographic **topoi**). |
| **Hermes** **psychopomp** **in** **comic** **/** **mythic** **scenes** | **Aristophanes** *Frogs*; **overlap** with **underworld** **journeys** — [Hermes — mediator and initiation](hermes-mediator-and-initiation.md). |
| **Dionysus** **eastward** **mission** **motif** | **Euripides** *Bacchae*; **Diodorus** *Bibliotheca historica* (literary **topos**; **not** **history** of **India** **mission** without **argument**). |
| **Orpheus** | **Apollonius** *Argonautica*; **Orphic**-**labeled** **hymns** and **fragments** — [Greco-Roman Dionysus](../figures/greco-roman-dionysus.md), appendix §4. |
| **Attis** **and** **Cybele** | **Pausanias**; **Diodorus** — [Attis and Cybele](attis-and-cybele.md). |
| **Delphi** **foundation** | *Homeric Hymn to Apollo*; **Pausanias**. |
| **Late** **Hermes** **Trismegistus** | *Corpus Hermeticum* etc. — **distinct** from **archaic** **Hermes** **cult**; **chronology** **matters** for **comparison**. |
| **Pythagoras** **as** **sage**-**traveler** | **Iamblichus** *Life of Pythagoras* (late **biographical** **tradition**). |

**Caution:** **Phrases** like “**similar** to **baptism**” for **Attis** **rites** are **comparative** **shorthand**; **institutions** and **meanings** **differ** from **Christian** **baptism** **proper**.

## 3. Chronological spine — primary and paratextual witnesses

Approximate **periods**; **titles** **standard** in **Anglophone** **surveys**.

### Early (8th–6th cent. BCE)

- **Homeric Hymns** (incl. **Demeter**, **Apollo**, **Hermes**, **Dionysus**).
- **Hesiod**, *Theogony*; *Works and Days*.

### Archaic–Classical (6th–4th cent. BCE)

- **Orphic** **gold** **tablets** (funerary **instructions**; **afterlife** **topography**).
- **Derveni** **papyrus** (commentary on **Orphic**-**theogonic** **material**).
- **Pindar** **epinicians** (incl. **afterlife** **hints** in **Olympian** 2 — **debated**).
- **Tragedy**: **Aeschylus**, **Sophocles**, **Euripides** (*Bacchae*, **etc.**).
- **Herodotus** *Histories* (cross-**cultural** **ritual** **reporting**).

### Hellenistic (4th–1st cent. BCE)

- **Callimachus** **hymns**; **Apollonius** *Argonautica*.
- ***Orphic Hymns*** (87 **hymns**; **manuscript** **tradition** **late**; **ritual** **prescripts** **often** **included**).

### Roman Imperial (1st cent. BCE–4th cent. CE)

- **Ovid** *Metamorphoses*; **Plutarch** *Moralia* (e.g. *On Isis and Osiris*; *On the E at Delphi*).
- **Pausanias** *Description of Greece*.
- **Clement** *Protrepticus*; **Hippolytus** *Refutation* (polemical **quotations** of **rival** **rites**).
- **Orphic** **Rhapsodies** (**fragments** via **Neoplatonist** **excerpts**).

### Late antiquity (4th–6th cent. CE)

- **Proclus** **commentaries**; **Damascius** *On First Principles*.

## 4. Orphic texts: what “instructions” can mean

**Specialists** **dispute** what is **“Orphic”** **in** **antiquity** vs **later** **labeling**. In **general**:

- **Purification**: **abstention**, **washings** (evidence **scattered**; **Derveni** **papyrus** **relevant**).
- **Hymnic** **performance**: ***Orphic Hymns*** **prescribe** **offerings** **and** **epithets** in **some** **manuscripts** — **not** **uniform** **across** **sites**.
- **Afterlife**: **gold** **tablets** **give** **soul**-**routes** and **passwords**; **Edmonds**, **Bernabé** **corpora** **are** **starting** **points**.
- **Cosmogony**: **Rhapsodies** **fragments**; **Neoplatonist** **filters**.
