# Chronological map

This page presents **three** complementary tables:

1. **Messianic and restorer figures** — the site's core comparison (future saviors, cosmic renovators)
2. **Mediator, savior, and renewal archetypes** — adjacent figures who carry "savior" language without being future eschatological messiahs
3. **Imperial contact zones and textual milestones** — the political contexts and documentary evidence that connect (or separate) the traditions above

Dates are approximate; see each tradition page for nuance. **Attestation ≠ origin**: oral traditions and lost texts mean many concepts are older than their first surviving written witness.

---

## 1. Messianic and restorer figures

The "classic" comparison set: figures whose primary role is **future cosmic restoration, judgment, or renewal**.

| Approx. first attestation | Tradition | Figure | Primary sources | Key motifs | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ~1000–600 BCE (seeds); ~500–300 BCE (named) | **Zoroastrianism** | [Saoshyant](../figures/zoroastrian-saoshyant.md) | Gathas; Younger Avesta (Yasht 13, Yasna 59); Bundahišn | Resurrection, judgment (molten metal), defeat of evil, Frashokereti | Earliest attested **future world-renovator** in Eurasia; three Saoshyants in Pahlavi elaboration |
| ~8th–6th cent. BCE (prophetic); ~2nd cent. BCE (apocalyptic) | **Judaism** | [Mashiach](../figures/judaism-mashiach.md) | Isaiah 11; Jeremiah; Daniel 7; Dead Sea Scrolls | Davidic king, Temple restoration, judgment, peace | Cosmic/eschatological dimension intensifies **during and after** Persian period |
| ~539 BCE | **Judaism / Persian** | **Cyrus as *meshiach*** | **Isaiah 45:1** | Divine anointing of a foreign king | A **Persian emperor** called "anointed" (*mashiach*) in a Jewish prophetic oracle — [Achaemenid page](achaemenid-and-west-asian-judaism.md) |
| ~3rd cent. BCE (Pāli Canon) | **Buddhism** | [Maitreya / Metteyya](../figures/buddhism-maitreya.md) | DN 26 (*Cakkavatti-Sīhanāda*); Maitreya sūtras | Future Buddha, golden age, dharma restoration | Possible distant Indo-Iranian influence (debated); elaborated in Mahāyāna and Central Asia |
| ~400 BCE–200 CE (epic core) | **Hinduism** | [Kalki](../figures/hinduism-kalki.md) | Mahābhārata 3.188–189; Purāṇas | White horse, sword, end of Kali Yuga, restoration of dharma | Avatāra theology; **yuga** cycle framing |
| ~165 BCE | **Judaism** (apocalyptic) | **"Son of Man"** (Daniel 7) | Daniel 7:13–14 | Heavenly figure given dominion; judgment scene with "Ancient of Days" | Enormously influential on Christian Christology; debated identity (angel? Israel personified? individual?) |
| ~2nd–1st cent. BCE | **Judaism** (Qumran) | **Messianic figures in DSS** | 4Q521 (messiah raises the dead); 1QSa (messianic banquet); 11Q13 (Melchizedek) | Resurrection, eschatological banquet, heavenly priest-king | Multiple concurrent messianic models; [DSS dualism page](dead-sea-scrolls-dualism-and-iranian-parallels.md) |
| ~67 BCE–1st cent. CE | **Roman Mithraism** | [Mithras](../figures/roman-mithraism.md) | Plutarch; Statius; tauroctony monuments | Initiation, cosmic mediation, oath/bond | **Present** soteriology; not primarily future eschatological; Iranian name, debated origins |
| ~50–100 CE | **Christianity** | [Jesus / Parousia](../figures/christianity-parousia.md) | NT (Gospels, Paul, Revelation) | Messiah, resurrection, second coming, judgment, new creation | Inherits and transforms Jewish messianism in Greco-Roman milieu |
| ~3rd cent. CE | **Manichaeism** | **Jesus of Light / Third Messenger** | Mani's epistles; Kephalaia; Iranian fragments | Light-savior, awakening of divine sparks, cosmic repair | Explicit **Iranian-Jewish-Buddhist-Christian** synthesis; [Gnosticism page](gnosticism-and-mandaeanism.md) |
| ~4th cent. CE (364–370 CE) | **Taoism** | [Li Hong 李弘](../figures/taoism-li-hong.md) | Shangqing revelations (*Zhen'gao*); Han prophetic lore | Sage-king, end-of-kalpa renewal, "seed people" | Chinese Daoist messianism; partly influenced by Buddhist kalpa discourse |
| ~7th–9th cent. CE | **Islam** | [Mahdi](../figures/islam-mahdi.md) | Hadith (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim, Sunan Abū Dāwūd); Shīʿī texts | Just ruler, justice before Judgment Day; Jesus (ʿĪsā) returns | Paired with ʿĪsā in many Sunni accounts; Twelfth Imam in Shīʿa |
| ~10th cent. CE (poem); 13th cent. mss | **Norse** | [Baldr](../figures/norse-baldr.md) | *Völuspá* (Poetic Edda) | Returns after Ragnarök; renewed world | Scholars debate Christian influence on eschatological stanzas |
| ~1125 CE | **Arthurian** | [King Arthur](../figures/arthurian-king-arthur.md) | William of Malmesbury; Geoffrey of Monmouth | Sleeping hero; return in hour of need | National restoration; medieval British prophetic tradition |

## 2. Mediator, savior, and renewal archetypes

Figures who carry **"savior," "healer," "initiator,"** or **"dying-and-returning"** language — important for understanding the vocabulary shared with messianic figures, even though they are not future eschatological restorers in the Jewish/Zoroastrian sense.

| Approx. date | Tradition | Figure | Primary sources | Key motifs | Site page |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ~2400 BCE (Pyramid Texts) | **Egyptian** | **Osiris** | Pyramid Texts; Coffin Texts; Plutarch *De Iside* | Death, dismemberment, resurrection as lord of dead; kingship succession | [Isis, Osiris, Serapis](isis-osiris-serapis.md) |
| ~1300 BCE (Ugaritic tablets) | **Canaanite** | **Baal** (Haddu) | KTU 1.1–1.6 (Baal Cycle) | Storm god defeats Sea (Yamm) and Death (Mot); dies and returns; kingship | [PIE and ANE traces in the Bible](pie-and-ane-traces-in-biblical-tradition.md) |
| ~1185 BCE (papyrus) | **Egyptian** | **Bata** (*Tale of Two Brothers*) | Papyrus d'Orbiney | Death, magical revival, transformation, return as king | [Two brothers and fraternal archetypes](pie-twin-and-brother-archetypes.md) |
| PIE reconstruction | **Indo-European** | **\*Manu / \*Yemo** | Reconstructed from Vedic Yama, Norse Ymir, Roman Remus, etc. | Cosmogonic sacrifice — one brother's body becomes the world | [Two brothers](pie-twin-and-brother-archetypes.md) |
| ~8th–5th cent. BCE | **Greek** | **Dionysus** | Homeric Hymns; Euripides *Bacchae*; Orphic texts | Wandering god, ecstasy, mysteries, dismemberment/return, afterlife hope | [Dionysus](../figures/greco-roman-dionysus.md) |
| ~6th cent. BCE onward | **Greek** | **Asclepius** | Epidaurus inscriptions; Pindar; Pausanias | Healing, incubation, dream epiphany, *sōtēr* epithet, serpent staff | [Asclepius](../figures/greco-roman-asclepius.md) |
| ~5th cent. BCE onward | **Greek** | **Heracles** (apotheosis) | Pindar; Attic tragedy; cult sites | Mortal hero → divine; labors, suffering, ascent to Olympus | [Greek wandering gods](greek-wandering-gods-initiation.md) |
| ~5th cent. BCE | **Greek** | **Dioscuri** (Castor & Polydeuces) | Homeric Hymn 33; Pindar *Nemean* 10; cult across Mediterranean | Divine twins; shared immortality; rescue; IE twin pattern | [Two brothers](pie-twin-and-brother-archetypes.md) |
| Hellenistic / Roman | **Egyptian-Greek** | **Serapis** | Ptolemaic creation; temple at Alexandria | Composite chthonic-healing god; *sōtēr* language | [Isis, Osiris, Serapis](isis-osiris-serapis.md) |
| Hellenistic / Roman | **Phrygian-Roman** | **Attis** | Catullus 63; Ovid *Fasti*; Cybele cult | Self-castration, death, ritual mourning, spring festival | [Attis and Cybele](attis-and-cybele.md) |
| Roman imperial | **Roman** | **Emperor as *divus* / *sōtēr*** | Inscriptions, coins, provincial temples | Living ruler and deified predecessor; civic "savior" language | [Emperor cult](emperor-cult-and-civic-soter.md) |
| ~2nd–4th cent. CE | **Gnostic** | **Revealer / Savior of Light** | Nag Hammadi texts; *Apocryphon of John*; Mandaean *Ginza Rabba* | Descent to awaken divine sparks; *gnōsis* as salvation | [Gnosticism and Mandaeanism](gnosticism-and-mandaeanism.md) |

## 3. Imperial contact zones and textual milestones

This table asks: **when were traditions in the same political or geographic space?** Proximity does not prove borrowing, but it establishes the **mechanism** without which influence claims are empty.

### 3a. Empires and contact zones

| Period | Empire / zone | Traditions in contact | Key evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ~1400 BCE | **Mitanni** treaties | Indo-Aryan gods (Mitra, Varuṇa, Indra, Nasatya) invoked in Hittite treaties alongside Hurrian gods | [Indo-Iranian Mitra](indo-iranian-mitra-contract.md) |
| ~1300–1200 BCE | **Late Bronze Age** (Ugarit, Hattusa, Egypt) | Canaanite, Hittite, Egyptian mythologies in close contact; Baal Cycle composed | [PIE and ANE traces](pie-and-ane-traces-in-biblical-tradition.md) |
| **~550–330 BCE** | **Achaemenid empire** | **Zoroastrian, Jewish, Babylonian, Egyptian, Greek** — all under one administration | [Achaemenid page](achaemenid-and-west-asian-judaism.md); [PFT and Royal Road](evidence-leads-persian-milieu-and-mitra-cluster.md) |
| ~539 BCE | Cyrus conquers Babylon | Jews encounter Persian imperial religion; Cyrus called *meshiach* (Isa 45:1); temple restoration | [Loanwords](persian-loanwords-in-biblical-tradition.md) |
| ~525 BCE | Cambyses conquers Egypt | Elephantine Jewish community under Persian rule; Aramaic papyri | [Achaemenid page](achaemenid-and-west-asian-judaism.md) |
| ~330–150 BCE | **Hellenistic** kingdoms | Greek, Iranian, Jewish, Egyptian, Babylonian cultures under Seleucid/Ptolemaic rule | Translation (LXX); Greek philosophical influence on Judaism |
| **~247 BCE–224 CE** | **Parthian empire** | **Jewish (Babylonian), Iranian, early Christian, Greco-Roman** on borders; Kushan to the east | [Parthian corridor](parthian-arsacid-corridor.md) |
| ~2nd cent. BCE–1st cent. CE | **Indo-Greek / Saka / Kushan** | **Buddhist, Greek, Iranian** in Gandhāra; Maitreya imagery develops | [Gandhāra page](gandhara-central-asian-transmission.md) |
| ~1st–3rd cent. CE | **Kushan empire** | Iranian, Buddhist, Greek deities on **same coins** (MIIRO, BODDO, NANA) | [Kushan coins](gandhara-central-asian-transmission.md) |
| ~1st–3rd cent. CE | **Roman empire** (eastern provinces) | Jewish, Christian, Mithraic, Isiac, Dionysiac, civic emperor cult **in same cities** | [Philippi](philippi-via-egnatia-religious-milieu.md); [Dura-Europos](parthian-arsacid-corridor.md) |
| ~256 CE (destruction) | **Dura-Europos** | Synagogue + Mithraeum + Christian house-church **in one city** | [Parthian corridor](parthian-arsacid-corridor.md) |
| ~216–277 CE | **Mani's mission** | Explicit synthesis of Zoroastrian, Christian, Buddhist elements in Parthian Mesopotamia | [Gnosticism page](gnosticism-and-mandaeanism.md); [Manichaean Mihr](manichaean-mihr-soteriology.md) |
| ~224–651 CE | **Sasanian empire** | Zoroastrian "orthodoxy"; Jewish Babylonian Talmud compiled; Manichaeism persecuted; Nestorian Christianity tolerated | Late Iranian religion codified |

## How to use this map

- **Rows are not rankings** of "originality" or "truth."
- **Attestation** is not the same as **origin of the idea** — oral and lost texts matter.
- Cross-links are **hypotheses** to be weighed with evidence, not assumptions.
- Table 1 (restorer figures) answers "**who**"; Table 2 (mediators) answers "**what else carried savior language**"; Table 3 (contact zones) answers "**when could they have met?**"
- The **cumulative** pattern — six major eschatological concepts entering Judaism during the Persian/post-Persian period — is detailed on the [Eschatological vocabulary timeline](eschatological-vocabulary-timeline.md).
