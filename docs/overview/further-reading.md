## Proto-Indo-European reconstruction and Paleo-Balkan religion

- **Lincoln, Bruce.** *Myth, Cosmos, and Society: Indo-European Themes of Creation and Destruction* (Harvard, 1986) — sacrifice, kingship, comparative narrative.
- **Mallory, J. P. & Adams, D. Q.** *The Oxford Introduction to Proto-Indo-European and the Proto-Indo-European World*; *Encyclopedia of Indo-European Culture* — reference-level **PIE** context.
- **West, M. L.** *Indo-European Poetry and Myth* — narrative and deity patterns.
- Wikipedia (orientation): [*\*Manu and \*Yemo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manu_and_Yemo), [Divine twins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_twins), [Proto-Indo-European mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_mythology).
- **Thracian horseman:** [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thracian_horseman) — portal to **Toporov**, **Georgiev**, **Duridanov**, and epigraphic literature.
- **Site pages:** [Two brothers, divine twins, and fraternal archetypes](/docs/overview/pie-twin-and-brother-archetypes), [PIE and ANE traces in the Bible](/docs/overview/pie-and-ane-traces-in-biblical-tradition), [PIE motifs, ATU / Thompson, and hero traditions](/docs/overview/pie-motifs-atu-thompson-and-hero-traditions), [Greek wandering gods](/docs/overview/greek-wandering-gods-initiation), [Thracian horseman](/docs/overview/thracian-horseman-paleo-balkan).

## Mediterranean mystery cult and Philippi (Christian–pagan interface)

- **Burkert, Walter.** *Ancient Mystery Cults* (Harvard, 1987) — Eleusis, **Dionysus**, **Cybele/Attis**, comparative frame.
- **Turcan, Robert.** *The Cults of the Roman Empire* (Blackwell, 1996; tr.) — **Isis**, **Mithras**, **Cybele**, **Sol**.
- **Beard, Mary; North, John; Price, Simon.** *Religions of Rome* (Cambridge, 1998) — **state cult**, **sōtēr** idiom, **urban** religion.
- **Abrahamsen, Valerie A.** *Women and Worship at Philippi: Diana/Artemis and Other Cults in the Early Christian Era.* Astarte Shell Press, 1995 — **Artemis**, **Heros**, healing, **Dionysiac** epigraphy.
- **Collart, Paul.** *Philippes: Ville de Macédoine…* Paris, 1937 — excavation and cultic **Philippi**.
- **Portefaix, Lilian.** *Sisters Rejoice.* Uppsala: Coniectanea Biblica, 1988 — **women** and **Pauline** context.
- **Hoddinott, R. F.** *The Thracians.* Thames and Hudson, 1981 — **regional** religion.
- **Perdrizet, Paul.** “Inscriptions de Philippes: Les Rosalies,” *BCH* 24 (1900).
- **Hammond, N. G. L.** “Via Egnatia,” *Oxford Classical Dictionary*, 2nd ed. (1970), 1118 — **Pauline** travel corridor.
