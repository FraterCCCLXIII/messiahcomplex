# Bibliography

## Primary Sources

### Ancient Persian and Achaemenid

- **Behistun Inscription** (DB). Darius I, c. 520 BCE. Trilingual (Old Persian, Elamite, Babylonian). Critical edition: Schmitt, Rüdiger. *The Bisitun Inscriptions of Darius the Great: Old Persian Text*. SOAS, 1991.
- **Avesta**. Zoroastrian sacred corpus; compiled over centuries. Key texts: Gathas (hymns attributed to Zoroaster), Yasna (liturgy), Yashts (hymns), Vendidad (law code).
- **Persepolis Fortification Tablets**. Achaemenid administrative records, c. 509–494 BCE. Ed. Hallock, Richard T. *Persepolis Fortification Tablets*. University of Chicago Press, 1969.

### Greek Sources

- **Herodotus**. *Histories*. c. 440 BCE. Trans. Godley, A.D. Loeb Classical Library. Harvard University Press.
- **Ctesias**. *Persica*. 4th century BCE. Survives in Photius's summary (*Bibliotheca*, cod. 72). Trans. Lenfant, Dominique. *Ctésias de Cnide: La Perse, L'Inde, Autres Fragments*. Les Belles Lettres, 2004.
- **Xenophon**. *Cyropaedia*. 4th century BCE. Idealized account of Cyrus; not reliable biography.

### Jewish Sources

- **Hebrew Bible / Old Testament**. Relevant texts: Isaiah 40–55 (Deutero-Isaiah, Persian period), Daniel (Maccabean period), Ezra–Nehemiah, Esther.
- **Josephus**. *Antiquities of the Jews*. c. 93 CE. Trans. Whiston, William. *The Works of Josephus*. Hendrickson, 1987.
- **Dead Sea Scrolls**. 2nd century BCE–1st century CE. Translation: Vermes, Geza. *The Complete Dead Sea Scrolls in English*. Penguin, 2004.

### Buddhist Sources

- **Pāli Canon** (Tipiṭaka). Oral tradition from 5th–4th century BCE; committed to writing c. 1st century BCE (Sri Lanka). Accessible through Pāli Text Society translations and Bhikkhu Bodhi's translations (Wisdom Publications).
- **Aśokan Edicts**. c. 260–232 BCE. Inscriptions on pillars and rocks across the Mauryan Empire. Ed. Hultzsch, E. *Inscriptions of Asoka*. Oxford, 1925.

### New Testament

- Greek NT ed. Nestle–Aland (Novum Testamentum Graece), 28th ed., Deutsche Bibelgesellschaft, 2012.
- Standard English translation: NRSV or ESV.

## Secondary Sources

### Achaemenid Empire

- **Briant, Pierre**. *From Cyrus to Alexander: A History of the Persian Empire*. Trans. Daniels, Peter. Eisenbrauns, 2002. [Standard comprehensive reference]
- **Kuhrt, Amélie**. *The Persian Empire: A Corpus of Sources from the Achaemenid Period*. 2 vols. Routledge, 2007.
- **Waters, Matt**. *Ancient Persia: A Concise History of the Achaemenid Empire, 550–330 BCE*. Cambridge University Press, 2014.
- **Schmitt, Rüdiger**. "Bardiya." *Encyclopaedia Iranica*, Vol. III/7, pp. 756–758. [Key reference for the historical Bardiya]

### Scythian and Saka Studies

- **Cunliffe, Barry**. *The Scythians: Nomad Warriors of the Steppe*. Oxford University Press, 2019.
- **Harmatta, János** (ed.). *History of Civilizations of Central Asia, Volume II*. UNESCO, 1994.
- **Parpola, Asko**. "Proto-Dorian Movements and the Rig-Vedic Evidence." *Studia Orientalia*, 2008. [On Dāṇḍu/Iranian migrations into India]

### Axial Age Studies

- **Jaspers, Karl**. *The Origin and Goal of History*. Trans. Bullock, Michael. Yale University Press, 1953. [Coined "Axial Age"]
- **Bellah, Robert N.** *Religion in Human Evolution: From the Paleolithic to the Axial Age*. Harvard University Press, 2011.
- **Eisenstadt, S.N.** (ed.). *The Origins and Diversity of Axial Age Civilizations*. State University of New York Press, 1986.

### Zoroastrianism

- **Boyce, Mary**. *A History of Zoroastrianism*. 3 vols. Brill, 1975–1991. [Standard comprehensive reference]
- **Stausberg, Michael**. *Zarathustra and Zoroastrianism*. Equinox, 2008.
- **Skjærvø, Prods Oktor**. "Zoroastrianism." *Encyclopaedia Iranica*. [Freely available online; essential reference]

### Buddhism

- **Gombrich, Richard**. *Theravada Buddhism: A Social History from Ancient Benares to Modern Colombo*. Routledge, 2006.
- **Lamotte, Étienne**. *History of Indian Buddhism: From the Origins to the Śaka Era*. Trans. Webb-Boin, Sara. Peeters Press, 1988.
- **Schopen, Gregory**. *Bones, Stones, and Buddhist Monks*. University of Hawaii Press, 1997. [Critical use of archaeological evidence]
- **Attwood, Jayarava**. "Possible Iranian Origins for the Śākyas and Aspects of Buddhism." *Journal of the Oxford Centre for Buddhist Studies*, 2012. [Key reference for Śākya-Iranian hypothesis]

### Judaism and Christianity

- **Collins, John J.** *The Apocalyptic Imagination: An Introduction to Jewish Apocalyptic Literature*. 3rd ed. Eerdmans, 2016.
- **Sanders, E.P.** *Judaism: Practice and Belief, 63 BCE–66 CE*. SCM Press, 1992.
- **Fredriksen, Paula**. *When Christians Were Jews: The First Generation*. Yale University Press, 2018.
- **Hengel, Martin**. *Judaism and Hellenism*. 2 vols. Fortress Press, 1974. [On Hellenistic influence on Second Temple Judaism]

### Comparative Religion and Transmission

- **Beck, Roger**. *The Religion of the Mithras Cult in the Roman Empire*. Oxford University Press, 2006.
- **Burkert, Walter**. *The Orientalizing Revolution: Near Eastern Influence on Greek Culture in the Early Archaic Age*. Harvard University Press, 1992.
- **Boyce, Mary, and Grenet, Frantz**. *A History of Zoroastrianism, Vol. III: Zoroastrianism Under Macedonian and Roman Rule*. Brill, 1991.

### Linguistic Studies

- **Mayrhofer, Manfred**. *Etymologisches Wörterbuch des Altindoarischen* (EWAia). 3 vols. Carl Winter, 1992–2001. [Standard reference for Sanskrit etymology]
- **Cheung, Johnny**. *Etymological Dictionary of the Iranian Verb*. Brill, 2007.
- **Mallory, J.P. and Adams, D.Q.** *The Oxford Introduction to Proto-Indo-European and the Proto-Indo-European World*. Oxford University Press, 2006.
- **Watkins, Calvert**. *How to Kill a Dragon: Aspects of Indo-European Poetics*. Oxford University Press, 1995.

### Gandhara and Central Asia

- **Errington, Elizabeth, and Cribb, Joe**. *The Crossroads of Asia: Transformation in Image and Symbol*. Ancient India and Iran Trust, 1992.
- **Salomon, Richard**. *Indian Epigraphy*. Oxford University Press, 1998. [On Kharoṣṭhī and Brāhmī scripts]
