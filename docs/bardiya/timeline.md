# Timeline

## Pre-Achaemenid Period (Before 550 BCE)

### 2000–1500 BCE
- Spread of Proto-Indo-European (PIE) speakers across Eurasia
- Separation of Iranian and Indic branches of Indo-Iranian languages
- Composition of early Rigvedic material

### c. 1200–900 BCE
- Composition of the earliest Vedic texts (Rigveda)
- Probable period for composition of the Gathas (Zoroaster's hymns) — linguistic dating favors this range over later classical proposals

### c. 1000–800 BCE
- Scythian/Saka presence on Pontic steppe documented
- Development of kurgan burial traditions

### c. 850 BCE
- Abrupt climate shift (van Geel et al. 2004): increased humidity on steppe; drought in northwest India
- Associated with rapid Scythian/Saka expansion and possible migration pressure toward India

### c. 800–600 BCE
- Saka expansion eastward; contact with Iranian plateau
- Yautiya/Dāṇḍu tribal migrations into India proposed by Parpola (2008)
- Median kingdom consolidates in western Iran

## Achaemenid Empire Period (550–330 BCE)

### 559 BCE
- Cyrus II becomes King of Anshan (vassal under the Medes)

### 550 BCE
- Cyrus overthrows Astyages; establishes the Achaemenid Empire

### 547 BCE
- Cyrus captures Sardis; Lydia incorporated

### 539 BCE
- **Cyrus conquers Babylon** — removes the Neo-Babylonian Empire

### 538 BCE
- **Edict of Cyrus**: exiled peoples including Jews permitted to return to homelands and rebuild temples
- Isaiah 45:1 calls Cyrus *māšîaḥ* (messiah) — only non-Israelite with this title in the Hebrew Bible

### 530 BCE
- Cyrus dies in campaign against the Massagetae; Cambyses II succeeds

### 525 BCE
- Cambyses II conquers Egypt; Achaemenid Empire now spans three continents

### 522 BCE
- **Bardiya/Gaumata episode** — central date of the Bardiya hypothesis:
  - March: Gaumata claims throne as Bardiya while Cambyses is in Egypt
  - Gaumata rules; reportedly reduces taxes and military levies
  - Summer: Cambyses dies (location debated)
  - **September 29, 522 BCE**: Darius and six nobles kill Gaumata at Mount Nishaya in Media
  - Darius I seizes power; faces multiple revolts

### c. 520 BCE
- **Behistun Inscription** carved by Darius I — primary historical source for the Gaumata episode

### 518 BCE
- Scylax of Caryanda's expedition down the Indus River — documents Achaemenid interest in the far east

### 515 BCE
- **Second Temple completed** in Jerusalem under Persian sponsorship

### 513 BCE
- Darius's Scythian expedition across the Danube

### 500–479 BCE
- Ionian Revolt; Persian Wars with Greece

### 486 BCE
- Death of Darius I; Xerxes I succeeds

### 480–479 BCE
- Xerxes's invasion of Greece; Persian defeat at Salamis and Plataea

### 458 BCE
- Ezra's mission to Jerusalem — further Persian-sponsored legal reform of Jewish community

### 445 BCE
- Nehemiah rebuilds Jerusalem walls under Artaxerxes I's authorization

## Religious Developments of the Persian Period

| Date | Development | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| 539–538 BCE | Cyrus as messiah (Isaiah 45:1) | A |
| 515 BCE | Second Temple completed | A |
| c. 500–400 BCE | Zoroastrian eschatology entering Jewish thought | B |
| 5th–4th c. BCE | Buddha's life (revised dating: death c. 400–350 BCE) | B |
| c. 520–500 BCE | Behistun Inscription; Darius's propaganda | A |
| 4th c. BCE | Early Pāli Buddhist canon in oral transmission | A |

## Hellenistic Period (330–30 BCE)

### 334–323 BCE
- Alexander the Great conquers the Achaemenid Empire
- Persepolis sacked (330 BCE)
- Gandhara, Bactria, northwest India come under Macedonian/Greek control

### 323 BCE
- Death of Alexander; empire divided among the Diadochi

### 305 BCE
- Seleucid Empire established; controls Persia, Mesopotamia, Bactria

### c. 300–250 BCE
- Chandragupta Maurya's empire (324–297 BCE); Aśoka (r. c. 268–232 BCE) patronizes Buddhism
- Maitreya first mentioned in Pāli canonical texts (Cakkavatti Sīhanāda Sutta)

### c. 250 BCE
- Greco-Bactrian Kingdom breaks away; Parthian Empire begins

### c. 200–100 BCE
- Indo-Greek kingdoms in Gandhara and Punjab (Menander I Soter, c. 165–130 BCE)
- Early Buddhist stūpas constructed; Gandharan artistic synthesis begins
- Development of Mahāyāna Buddhism in this cultural zone

### 63 BCE
- Pompey conquers Jerusalem; Judea under Roman influence

---

## Roman Period (30 BCE–400 CE)

### 1st century BCE – 1st century CE
- Spread of Mithraism through Roman military
- Jesus of Nazareth active in Judea (traditional dates: c. 4 BCE–30 CE)

### c. 50–100 CE
- Paul's epistles (c. 50–58 CE); Canonical Gospels composed (70–100 CE)
- Early Christian movement spreads through the Roman Empire

### 1st–3rd centuries CE
- Kushan Empire in Central Asia; major patronage of Buddhism
- Gandharan art at its peak; Buddha images with solar halos
- Amitābha and Maitreya fully articulated in Mahāyāna texts
- Mani (216–274 CE) founds Manichaeism — synthesizes Zoroastrian, Buddhist, and Christian elements
